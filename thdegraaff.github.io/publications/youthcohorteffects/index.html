Varying Youth Cohort Effects on Regional Labour Market Outcomes in Germany

Thomas de Graaff, Ceren Ozgen, Duncan Roth
(2018) IZA discussion paper series, IZA DP no. 11932

Online  pdf

We assess how changes in youth cohort sizes effect employment rates in German labour market regions. Replicating the conventional approach, we estimate that a percentage increase in the youth share reduces regional employment rates by âˆ’0.2%. We challenge the assumption that cohort size effects are homogenous across space and find robust evidence that the negative effect of youth cohort size is more pronounced in the labour markets of metropolitan regions. These results suggest an upward pressure on urban regional employment rates as a result of the projected decrease in the size of the German youth share.
